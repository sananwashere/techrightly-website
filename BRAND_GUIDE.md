# TechRightly Brand Guide

## Positioning

TechRightly provides senior technology leadership and advisory to companies that need it without paying for a full-time hire: Fractional CTO, AI Strategy & Advisory, Solution Architecture, Digital Transformation, and Technical Due Diligence.

**Tagline:** Senior technology leadership — without the full-time overhead.

**Voice:** Confident, plain-spoken, senior-advisor. No hype, no jargon for its own sake. Write the way an experienced CTO talks to a founder: direct, specific, practical. Back claims with reasoning, not buzzwords.

**Audience:** Founders and execs at startups/scale-ups who need technical leadership but aren't ready for a full-time CTO; investors and acquirers who need technical due diligence on a target company.

## Color Palette

| Role | Name | Hex |
|---|---|---|
| Primary | Brand Navy | `#0B1F3A` |
| Secondary | Deep Indigo | `#1E3A8A` |
| Accent | Signal Teal | `#14B8A6` |
| Text | Slate 900 | `#0F172A` |
| Muted text | Slate 500 | `#64748B` |
| Background | Slate 50 | `#F8FAFC` |
| Border | Slate 200 | `#E2E8F0` |
| Surface | White | `#FFFFFF` |

Navy anchors the brand (header, footer, hero backgrounds). Teal is reserved for calls-to-action, links, and highlights so it stays meaningful. Indigo is a secondary accent for gradients/hover states.

## Typography

- **Headings:** Sora (geometric, technical, confident) — loaded via Google Fonts.
- **Body:** Inter (clean, highly readable at small sizes) — loaded via Google Fonts.

## Logo

`public/logo.svg` — horizontal lockup: a rounded-square mark (navy-to-teal gradient) containing a check/arrow glyph, paired with the "TechRightly" wordmark in Sora.

`public/icon.svg` — mark only, used for the favicon and social profile avatars.

These are simple, original placeholder marks suitable for launch. Treat them as a v1 — revisit with a designer once the brand has traction if a more distinctive identity is wanted.

## Usage Notes

- Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the mark.
- Don't recolor the mark outside the navy/teal gradient.
- Use Brand Navy on light backgrounds, white wordmark on navy/dark backgrounds.
